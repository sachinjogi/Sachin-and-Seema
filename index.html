Events:

Venue: Gomati Kalyana Mantapa, Hassan

22nd April

Sangeeth 7PM - 10PM

In Indian weddings, the Sangeet is a customary pre-wedding ceremony where the bride and groom exchange rings. It's a joyous occasion where family and friends from both sides come together to celebrate. During the event, guests perform songs, dances, and play games to mark the happy occasion. The festivities are then followed by a delicious dinner.

23rd April

Wedding 9AM-2PM

The wedding ceremony itself typically involves the tying of a sacred thread between the bride and groom, as well as the exchange of flower garlands and other symbolic items. This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.

Venue: Shamili, Udupi

25th April

Reception 11AM - 2PM

The Indian reception is a grand celebration that follows the wedding ceremony. It's a joyous occasion where family and friends come together to congratulate the newlyweds and enjoy food and music. It's a great opportunity for both families to mingle and make new memories.

Dress codes?

Hey there!
We want you to know that we care more about you being there to celebrate with us than what you wear to our wedding. So feel free to wear whatever makes you feel your best! But if you're looking for some ideas, we've got a few suggestions to share with you.

- Sangeeth : Indo-western
- Wedding : Ethnic Indian wear
- Reception : Indo-western

Ethnic Indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with dhoti/pajama along with an optional shawl.

Indo-western
Ladies can flaunt their sarees, lehengas, anarkalis, dresses and the list goes on whereas gentlemen can wear any kind of suit or just blazer. If you don't like to wear a suit/blazer, you can do a high-neck (band gala) waistcoat & shirt combo.
